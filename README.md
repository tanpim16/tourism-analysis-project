# 🗺️ Strategic Performance and Real Wealth Generation: A Data-Driven Portfolio Analysis of Thailand’s 55 Secondary Cities (2023–2025)

## **Project Overview**
This project addresses a critical economic question: **"Does increasing visitor volume in secondary cities actually translate into real wealth for local communities?"** We move beyond simple arrival counts to analyze **"Real Revenue"**—income adjusted by the Consumer Price Index (CPI)—to evaluate the true purchasing power and economic sustainability generated by Thailand's "Secondary City" tourism policy.

---

## **01 : Thailand's Tourism: The Volume-Value Paradox**
### *Moving Footprints vs. Moving Wealth*

Our first objective was to test the effectiveness of the redistribution policy. The data reveals a "Paradox of Plenty": we have successfully decentralized **Volume**, but **Value** remains concentrated in primary hubs.

* **The Success (Volume):** Secondary cities reached a peak of **11.2 million visitors** by late 2025.
* **The Struggle (Value):** Despite the crowds, secondary city revenue peaked at **35 Billion THB**, while primary hubs captured **248 Billion THB**.

## **05: Strategic Portfolio Analysis Results 📊**
### *Strategic Benchmarking: Classification by Yield and Weight*

We classified the 55 secondary cities using two critical dimensions: **Efficiency (Yield)** and **Market Share (Economic Weight)**.

### 📌 Analytical Framework
> **💡 Economic Accuracy:** All metrics are calculated using **Real Revenue** to ensure the analysis reflects true purchasing power.

$$Yield = \frac{\text{Real Revenue (Millions)} \times 1,000,000}{\text{Total Visitors (Persons)}}$$

$$\text{Market Share (\%)} = \frac{\text{Real Revenue of Province}}{\text{Total Real Revenue of 55 Secondary Cities}} \times 100$$

## **🚀 Strategic Recommendations**
1.  **For Stars:** Invest in premium hospitality to sustain high yields and market leadership.
2.  **For Premium Niche:** Target specific digital "interest tribes" 60 days in advance to convert intent into high-value visits.
3.  **For Mass Market:** Increase local "spending touchpoints" (workshops, high-quality souvenirs) to convert volume into real value.
4.  **For Emerging:** Build foundational accessibility and digital awareness to move toward higher quadrants.
